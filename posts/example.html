Just plain html. Keep it simple.1 1 This is a sidenote. It lives right here in the markup, inside the same paragraph as its reference, but floats out into the margin. The content stays in its column; the note appears beside it, right where you are reading.

Sidenotes beat footnotes because you never lose your place. Your eye catches the note at the moment it is relevant, then slides back to the sentence without any page-jump.2 2 Edward Tufte made this case in Envisioning Information (1990). His books are themselves the best demonstration of the principle. The reading experience stays linear even as the annotations multiply.

On small screens the sidenotes reflow into the main column as indented callouts, so nothing is ever hidden.
